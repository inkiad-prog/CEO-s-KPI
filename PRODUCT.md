# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users
Cluster leadership and department heads at Akij: the CEO and three other enroll-gated executives (Dashboard access), plus KPI-entry users acting in one of four roles per SBU — Finance, CEO's Office, CEO, or CBO/SBU. Entry users work monthly, in short sessions, filling in their assigned slice of a balanced scorecard for a specific business unit. Dashboard users check cluster-wide and per-SBU performance, and finalize/lock each month once all data is in.

## Product Purpose
CEO KPI replaces manual, scattered monthly reporting across 14 Akij business units with a single structured system: a fixed 19-KPI balanced scorecard (Financial, Customer, Internal Process, Learning & Growth) tracked per SBU per month. Each KPI has an accountable role that submits its actuals once (submission is then locked and immutable). The Dashboard shows live completion status across all SBUs, per-SBU and cluster-wide rollups (target, achievement, achievement %, weighted score), and finalizes the month once every SBU/role has submitted — at which point that month's results are locked and exportable as a PDF.

## Positioning
One locked, auditable monthly scorecard per business unit, rolled up into a single cluster-wide view the CEO can trust without chasing spreadsheets.

## Brand Personality
Sharp, precise, authoritative — an executive instrument, not a consumer app. Every number should read as trustworthy and final once locked; the interface should feel calm under a full 19×14 grid of data rather than dense or cluttered.

## Anti-references
Not a generic SaaS admin template (no purple gradients, no generic card-grid dashboard cliches). Not playful or consumer-styled. Avoid ambiguity about whether a number is a live draft or a locked, final figure.

## Design Principles
- Locked is a visible state, not just a backend flag — the UI must make submitted/locked data unmistakably different from editable drafts.
- Completion is the hero metric on the Dashboard before the CEO ever needs the scorecard detail — "12/14 SBUs done" matters as much as any single KPI.
- Every KPI entry carries its own context (strategic goal, direction, measurement approach, evidence requirements) inline, so entry users never need a separate reference document.
- Precision over decoration — this is where financial and operational figures get certified monthly; craft serves clarity and trust, not flourish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
No specific WCAG level requested yet; default to WCAG 2.1 AA (this is an internal executive tool handling financial data, and standard contrast/keyboard/labeling practices apply by default).
